# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Malaysian school principals and senior administrators at SMK-level (secondary) public schools. They log in at the start of the school day, often on a desktop workstation in an office environment, occasionally on a shared laptop. Their primary mode is scanning — they want to know in under 30 seconds whether anything needs attention. Secondary use: drilling into a specific module (fees, attendance, at-risk) when something flags.

## Product Purpose

EduSync is the administrative operating system for a Malaysian school. It aggregates enrolment, attendance, academic performance, fee collection, staffing, and communications into one screen so a principal can run a school without switching between multiple ministry systems. Success is a principal who feels genuinely in control of their institution — not buried in data, not hunting for information.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Local. Reliable. The product speaks like a trusted staff member, not a startup. It uses Malaysian conventions (RM, DD/MM/YYYY, BM terms where appropriate), carries no hype, and never uses emoji in product UI. Confident and specific — it shows the number, not a vague indicator.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (Notion, Linear)**: Too template-y, loses Malaysian institutional context. The product should feel grounded in a specific place and system, not interchangeable with any B2B tool.
- Avoid the "calm productivity tool" aesthetic — this is an operational command centre, not a note-taking app.

## Design Principles

1. **Scan before drill** — the overview screen answers the principal's morning question in one glance. No hunting required.
2. **Signal, not decoration** — Crimson is a signal (one primary action, one at-risk indicator). Every use of accent colour must earn its place.
3. **Malaysian by default** — RM currency, Malaysian grading, BM-ready labels, school-year calendar. Never feel like a US product localised.
4. **Institutional weight without heaviness** — the interface has authority (dark-first, structured typography, compressed data density) without being oppressive. It feels like a well-run ministry briefing, not a committee document.
5. **Earn every pixel** — admin dashboards bloat. Every added module or widget must justify its space against the scan-first principle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Keyboard navigable throughout. Screen reader support for all interactive regions. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for all count-up and chart animations. Color is never the sole channel for meaning (level pills use text labels, not just colour).
